# HR-Employee-Attrition-Analysis (Interactive Dashboard creation using Tableau)

## 🎯 Project Objective
The primary objective of this project is to analyze employee attrition to identify the key drivers behind why staff members leave the organization. By examining demographic, financial, and performance data, the project aims to provide actionable insights that help HR reduce turnover and improve employee retention.

## ❓ Business Questions
-To meet the objective, the analysis must answer:
-What is the overall Attrition Rate and how does it vary across departments?
-Which Job Roles are most at risk of leaving?
-Is there a correlation between Age Bands (e.g., 25–34) and the likelihood of quitting?
-Does Overtime or Work-Life Balance significantly impact the decision to leave?
-Are employees with lower Job Satisfaction or Monthly Income leaving at higher rates?

# 📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
These metrics will serve as the "health check" for the organization’s workforce:
1.	Attrition Count: Total number of employees who left the company (where Attrition = "Yes").
2. Attrition Rate: The percentage of the total workforce that has left.$$\text{Attrition Rate} = \left( \frac{\text{Total Attrition}}{\text{Total Employee Count}} \right) \times 100$$
3.	Average Age: To understand the demographic profile of the workforce.
4.	Average Tenure: How many years (on average) employees stay before leaving.
5.	Average Monthly Income: To benchmark compensation against turnover

## ⚙️ The Process
To go from raw data to a finished dashboard or report, follow these steps:
1. Data Cleaning & Preparation
•	Handle Missing Values: Ensure every employee record is complete.
•	Data Formatting: Ensure "Monthly Income" is numeric and "Employee Number" is unique.
•	Create Age Bands: Group ages (e.g., 18-24, 25-34) to see trends more clearly.
2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
•	Filter the data by Current Employees vs. Ex-Employees.
•	Compare "Years Since Last Promotion" against attrition to see if career stagnation is a factor.
3. Data Visualization
•	Heatmaps: To show attrition by Job Role and Age Band.
•	Bar Charts: To compare attrition across Departments (Sales, R&D, HR).
•	Donut Charts: To show the split of Male vs. Female attrition.
4. Insight Generation
•	Identify the "Pain Points" (e.g., "Sales Executives in the 25-34 age bracket have the highest attrition rate").
•	Recommend interventions (e.g., "Improve work-life balance for roles requiring frequent travel").
